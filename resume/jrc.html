The Orthorectification Server

Satellite images does not came out straight. They always appear distorted for different reasons such as the angle of the satellite camera and by the fact that Earth is not a perfect sphere (mountains).

The process of stretching an image to make it straight is called Orthorectification and it is usually done manually by an expert with the aid of a GIS software.

But at JRC we have an program able to do perform this orthorectification process by itself. I managed to get it working and exposing it as a web service to other our partners. The service was exposed using the OGC/WPS protocol, a standard interoperability protocol mainly used whitin the GIS comunity.
